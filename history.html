The History of Blue Oak Lake

Founded 1758

Blue Oak lake is a fictitious community located in the Catskill region of New York State.   It is surrounded by verdant forests, undulating mountains, swiftly moving streams and rivers, meandering creeks, waterfalls, and an abundance of wildlife.  The town land was originally purchased from the Algonquian Indians by William Penn in 1712.   It was subsequently settled by early Dutch and English colonists.

By 1789, the lake became mainly a recreational area frequented by urbanites from New York City.  Blue Oak Lake was and continues to be regularly stocked with fish and was noted for its abundance of bass, perch, pickerel, and bluegills.   Environmental visionaries have strived to protect the lake's pristine landscape despite continued development.
